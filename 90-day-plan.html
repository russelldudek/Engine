Entry Principle

Earn the right to standardize. Start with decisions and friction Engine already feels; prove a lighter operating mechanism on live work before scaling the centralized function.

Days 0-30 - Map the Real System

  • Interview CEO, Chief People Officer, Finance, Product, Sales, Operations, and initiative owners.
  • Trace annual planning, MBR/QBR, headcount allocation, initiative review, escalation, and decision-record flows.
  • Identify recurring ELT decisions, hidden work queues, stale dashboards, duplicate reporting, and commitments without protected capacity.
  • Map stakeholder topology and decision rights.
  • Select one or two pilot commitments with clear executive sponsorship and meaningful cross-functional friction.

Deliverables

  • Current-state operating map
  • Decision and meeting inventory
  • Portfolio and capacity hypothesis
  • Pilot-selection memo
  • Baseline measures and source-quality assessment

Decisions Required

Which initiatives enter the pilot; which executive forum will change; which data sources are authoritative; what the pilot may stop doing.

Days 31-60 - Install the Pilot Route

  • Create commitment contracts with outcome, DRI, capacity, dependencies, leading signals, finish condition, and pivot/stop trigger.
  • Build an async initiative-health pulse that names missing evidence rather than masking it.
  • Redesign one leadership forum around exceptions, tradeoffs, and decisions.
  • Automate collection and synthesis where data quality permits; require human review of decision packets.
  • Coach initiative owners and document friction.

Days 61-90 - Scale What Works

  • Assess pilot effect on decision latency, reporting burden, ownership clarity, resource fit, and intervention quality.
  • Codify reusable playbooks, decision rights, and exit criteria for centralized operators.
  • Connect company priorities to Finance and People planning.
  • Design the centralized Business Operations team structure and dynamic deployment model.
  • Publish the next-cycle operating calendar, adoption plan, and instrumentation backlog.

90-Day Outcomes

  • One working decision-dense executive cadence
  • A visible portfolio of explicit commitments and capacity assumptions
  • A minimum viable telemetry layer with disclosed data quality
  • A tested AI-assisted reporting workflow with human authority intact
  • A clear model for where centralized operators deploy, how they measure value, and when they exit
