Purpose

The Commitment Routing System creates a common language for enterprise initiatives without imposing a heavy methodology. It makes the minimum information required for a resource or intervention decision visible.

Commitment Contract

FieldDecision it supports
Outcome and finish conditionWhat must be observably true?
DRI and decision rightsWho owns delivery and which decisions remain executive?
Value hypothesisWhy is this worth protected capacity?
Resource envelopeWhich people, skills, money, and dependencies are actually committed?
Leading signalsWhat changes early enough to intervene?
Decision dateWhen must leadership decide, not merely review?
Pivot/stop triggerWhat evidence changes the route?

Operating Responses

  • Keep course: evidence, capacity, and execution are aligned.
  • Protect/sequence: value case is sound but capacity is overloaded.
  • Watch/bound: proceed with tighter signals and dated guardrails.
  • Reroute: execution health or dependencies require intervention.
  • Stop/reframe: evidence does not justify continued protected capacity.

Executive Scorecard

  • Portfolio focus: capacity tied to explicit enterprise outcomes.
  • Resource fit: gap between committed scope and funded specialist capacity.
  • Delivery pace: movement against dated outcome milestones.
  • Decision latency: time from surfaced variance to named decision.
  • Signal integrity: completeness, freshness, consistency, and ownership of evidence.
  • Intervention quality: whether decisions reduce recurrence, delay, rework, or hidden work.
  • Adoption: whether the operating mechanism is retained and used by initiative owners.

AI Assistance Boundaries

AI may collect, validate, reconcile, summarize, classify, draft, and preserve operating context. Humans retain authority over strategic priority, resource allocation, risk acceptance, performance judgment, and stopping decisions.

Meeting Design

Async packets arrive before the forum. The forum begins with exceptions and decision questions. Each decision ends with an owner, action, date, and updated commitment record. Status that does not require judgment stays out of the meeting.

Integrity Rules

  • No composite score without visible components and units.
  • No green status when required evidence is stale or missing.
  • No “priority” without named capacity tradeoffs.
  • No centralized operator without a defined exit condition.
