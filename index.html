01 / COMPANY MOMENT

Engine is scaling the company behind the platform.

The role appears at the moment when travel, payments, spend controls, approvals, groups, events, integrations, and customer growth create more enterprise interdependence. The operating system must mature without turning speed into bureaucracy.

The posting is unusually direct: Business Operations is the strategy and operating arm, not a support function. The leader owns planning cycles, resource allocation, execution infrastructure, ELT visibility, a centralized operator pool, and AI-driven leverage.

The actual mandate is therefore not to install a heavier planning framework. It is to shorten the distance between an executive decision and an observable result.

03 / OPERATING MODEL

Route commitments, not paperwork.

The Commitment Routing System makes every major initiative answer the same small set of decision-critical questions, then uses cadence and telemetry to keep the route honest.

01

Direction

Translate strategy into a few enterprise outcomes and explicit decision boundaries.

02

Commit

Define the outcome, DRI, value hypothesis, decision date, and finish condition.

03

Route

Match scope to headcount, specialist capacity, dependencies, and sequencing.

04

Observe

Collect leading signals asynchronously; make variance visible before the meeting.

05

Intervene

Use MBRs and QBRs for debate and decisions: proceed, protect, reroute, pause, or stop.

07 / FIRST 90 DAYS

Earn the right to standardize.

Start with the decisions and friction Engine already feels. Prove a lighter operating system on live work before scaling the centralized function.

Days 0-30

Map the real system

  • Interview CEO, CPO, Finance, Product, Sales, Operations, and initiative owners.
  • Trace planning, resource, escalation, and review flows.
  • Identify recurring decisions, hidden queues, and duplicate reporting.
  • Select one or two high-value pilot commitments.
Days 31-60

Install the pilot route

  • Define commitment contracts and async signal packets.
  • Reframe one executive forum around debate and decisions.
  • Build a minimum viable initiative-health pulse.
  • Use AI to reduce collection and synthesis work with human review.
Days 61-90

Scale what works

  • Codify decision rights, intervention patterns, and stop rules.
  • Design the centralized operator pool and deployment criteria.
  • Connect planning, headcount, and portfolio decisions.
  • Publish the next-cycle operating calendar and adoption plan.
08 / EXECUTIVE QUESTIONS

Questions that reveal the real mandate.

01Which decisions consume the most ELT time today, yet still recur because the underlying operating mechanism is weak?
02Where does resource allocation become implicit - through overloaded teams, silent tradeoffs, or work that is “priority” without protected capacity?
03Which company commitments can currently drift the longest before leadership receives a trustworthy signal?
04Where can a centralized operator add leverage without weakening functional ownership?
05After one full planning cycle, what must be visibly different for the CEO and ELT to say this function changed the pace of the business?
